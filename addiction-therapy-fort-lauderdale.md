## **CBT THERAPY: Rewiring Thoughts That Fuel Addiction**

**CBT THERAPY** (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) is one of the most effective
approaches in addiction recovery because it focuses on the connection between
thoughts, feelings, and actions.

Addiction often runs on autopilot:

  * “I can’t handle this stress.”
  * “One time won’t matter.”
  * “I already messed up, so why try?”

CBT helps us slow that down, challenge the belief, and replace it with a
healthier, more realistic response.

### **Real-Life CBT Tools We Practice**

We work on skills like:

  * Trigger identification and planning
  * Thought reframing
  * Urge surfing (riding cravings without acting)
  * Problem-solving under stress
  * Building routine and accountability

CBT is practical. It’s not just talking—it’s training.

## **EMDR Therapy: Reducing the Emotional Charge of Trauma**

[**EMDR therapy**](https://www.google.com/maps?cid=5174639524515557938) (Eye
Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is a specialized therapy that helps
people process traumatic memories so they become less intense and less
triggering.

For clients whose substance use is tied to trauma responses, EMDR can be a
game-changer. The goal isn’t to erase memory—it’s to reduce the emotional
“shockwave” that memory creates, so the nervous system doesn’t keep sounding
the alarm.

### **Why EMDR Supports Addiction Recovery**

When trauma memories lose their power, common relapse drivers often decrease:

  * Hypervigilance and anxiety
  * Sleep disruption
  * Emotional numbing
  * Sudden panic or shutdown responses

That emotional stability makes sobriety easier to maintain.

## **FAQs**

### **1) How do we know if we need addiction therapy in Fort Lauderdale?**

If substance use is affecting your relationships, health, work, finances, or
peace of mind—or if you’ve tried to stop and can’t—therapy is a smart next
step. You don’t need a “rock bottom” moment to deserve support.

### **2) Can mental health treatment happen at the same time as substance
abuse treatment?** **

**

Yes. In fact, it often should. When anxiety, depression, or trauma are treated
alongside addiction, recovery tends to be stronger and more sustainable.

### **3) What is medically assisted treatment, and is it right for everyone?**

Medically assisted treatment uses appropriate medications under medical
supervision to reduce cravings and support withdrawal management. It isn’t
necessary for everyone, but for some clients it can significantly improve
safety and comfort in early recovery.

### **4) How does CBT THERAPY help with cravings and relapse prevention?**

CBT THERAPY helps us identify trigger thoughts (“I can’t handle this”) and
replace them with healthier, more realistic responses. It also teaches
practical coping skills that work when cravings spike.

### **5) Is EMDR therapy only for people with severe trauma?** **

**

Not necessarily. EMDR therapy can help anyone whose past experiences still
feel emotionally “charged” in the present. When trauma-driven stress
decreases, many people find that cravings, anxiety, and relapse risk also
decrease.
