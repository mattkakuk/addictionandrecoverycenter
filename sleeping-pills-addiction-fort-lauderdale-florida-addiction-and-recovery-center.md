## **What Counts as “Sleeping Pills” (And Why It Matters)**

“Sleeping pills” is a catch-all phrase, but different medications affect the
body in different ways—and that matters when we’re building a safe recovery
plan.

### **Common Types of Sleep Medications**

These may include:

  * Sedative-hypnotics (“Z-drugs” often prescribed for sleep.
  * Benzodiazepines (sometimes prescribed for anxiety and sleep)
  * Certain prescription sedatives are used off-label
  * Over-the-counter sleep aids (which can still be misused)

#### **The Core Problem Isn’t the Label—It’s the Pattern**

Addiction isn’t defined by a brand name. It’s defined by loss of control:
taking more than intended, struggling to stop, and continuing even when it
causes harm.

## **Why Sleeping Pills Can Become Addictive So Quickly**

Sleep meds can feel like flipping a switch—until the switch stops working.

### **Tolerance: When “One” Turns Into “Two.”**

Over time, many people develop tolerance. The same dose that once knocked you
out now barely takes the edge off. That can lead to:

  * Taking extra doses
  * Taking them earlier
  * Mixing with alcohol (dangerous)
  * Doctor shopping or borrowing pills

#### **Dependence vs. Addiction**

  * **Dependence** means your body adapts, and withdrawal may happen if you stop.
  * **Addiction** means the behavior becomes compulsive, despite consequences.

We treat both—because both can derail your life.

## **Signs of Sleeping Pills Addiction We See in Fort Lauderdale Every Day**

People rarely wake up and decide to become dependent. It’s more like sinking
sand—slow, quiet, and shocking once you notice how deep you are.

### **Behavioral Signs**

  * Running out early or panicking when the supply is low
  * Taking pills “just in case” you can’t sleep
  * Hiding use from family or friends
  * Repeated failed attempts to cut back

### **Physical and Emotional Signs**

  * Brain fog, memory issues, or grogginess
  * Mood swings or irritability
  * Rebound insomnia (sleep gets worse without pills)
  * Anxiety spikes when you try to stop

#### **The Big Red Flag**

If sleep pills have become your _only_ way to function—at night or during the
day—we should talk.
