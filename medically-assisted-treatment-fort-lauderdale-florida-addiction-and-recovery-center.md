## **Evidence-Based Medications: What Options Exist and Why They Matter**

For opioid use disorder, federal health authorities recognize several FDA-
approved medications commonly used in treatment, including **buprenorphine** ,
**methadone** , and **naltrexone**.

### **Buprenorphine**

Often used to reduce cravings and withdrawal symptoms so you can stabilize and
engage in therapy.

### **Methadone**

A long-established option delivered under specific regulations and clinical
oversight models.

### **Naltrexone**

An option that works differently—commonly discussed for blocking opioid
effects and supporting relapse prevention for some individuals.

#### **The Bigger Picture**

Medication treatment for opioid use disorder has been associated with reduced
overdose risk and overall mortality—especially when paired with care supports.

Medication can help you breathe again—but therapy helps you _live_ again.

Sometimes it’s quiet and chronic—like carrying a backpack of rocks every day
